Flood Plains

A flood plain is landform formed by the deposition if eroded maerials and sediments from the upper course of a river to the lower course, especially when the river overflows its banks.

Gay lussac's law of Combining Volumes.

The law states that when gases react, they do so in volumes which bear simpler ratios to one another and to the volume of the products if gaseous, provided that the temperature and pressure are kept constant.
